# Validation Spreadsheet Guide

How to use the validation spreadsheet to track prospects and make data-driven decisions.

## Column Descriptions

### Basic Info
- **Company Name**: Target company
- **Contact Name**: Person you're reaching out to
- **Email**: Contact email
- **LinkedIn**: LinkedIn profile URL
- **Company Size**: Employees (50-500 target range)
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, E-commerce, Fintech, etc.

### Outreach Tracking
- **Outreach Date**: When you sent first email
- **Response Date**: When they replied
- **Call Booked**: Yes/No
- **Call Date**: Scheduled call date

### Validation Metrics (Most Important)
- **Pain Intensity (1-10)**: How urgent is their problem?
  - 1-3: Not a problem
  - 4-6: Minor annoyance
  - 7-8: Real pain, actively looking
  - 9-10: Critical, blocking growth

- **Current Support Spend ($/mo)**: Rough estimate
  - Helps calculate ROI
  - If they don't know, estimate: Team size × $8K-12K/month per person

- **Would Pay $500+/mo**: Yes/No/Maybe
  - **Yes**: Hot lead, proceed to MVP
  - **Maybe**: Warm, needs more validation
  - **No**: Cold, but still valuable feedback

- **Hot/Warm/Cold**: Overall lead quality
  - **Hot**: High pain (8+), would pay, interested in beta
  - **Warm**: Some pain (6-7), maybe would pay, needs more info
  - **Cold**: Low pain (<6), not interested, or no response

### Discovery Insights
- **Key Pain Points**: What they said (verbatim quotes)
  - Examples: "Volume is killing us", "Can't afford 24/7", "Team is burning out"

- **Must-Have Features**: Top 3 features they mentioned
  - These become your MVP features
  - Examples: "Zendesk integration", "Easy setup", "Accurate answers"

- **Objections/Concerns**: What would stop them from buying
  - Examples: "Accuracy concerns", "Security", "Setup complexity"
  - These are risks to address

- **Beta Interest**: Yes/No/Maybe
  - Yes = potential design partner
  - Track separately for beta recruitment

### Notes & Actions
- **Notes**: Any other context
  - Personal details, company specifics, etc.

- **Next Action**: What to do next
  - Examples: "Follow up in 2 weeks", "Send beta invite", "Add to warm list"

## How to Use This Data

### After 10 Calls: Pattern Recognition

1. **Calculate averages:**
   - Average pain intensity: _____
   - % who would pay $500+/mo: _____
   - Average support spend: $_____

2. **Identify patterns:**
   - Most common pain point: _____
   - Most requested feature: _____
   - Biggest objection: _____

3. **Segment by industry:**
   - Which industry shows most interest?
   - Which has highest pain?
   - Which has budget?

### Decision Rules (From Execution Guide)

**Proceed to MVP if:**
- ✅ 10+/20 say "Yes" to $500+/mo
- ✅ Average pain intensity > 7/10
- ✅ Clear pattern in must-have features
- ✅ At least 5 people interested in beta

**Pivot or adjust if:**
- ❌ Less than 5/20 say "Yes"
- ❌ Average pain < 6/10
- ❌ No clear feature pattern
- ❌ Everyone says "nice to have, not urgent"

### Week 1 Goal Tracking

**Target:** 20 validation calls booked from 50 outreach emails

**Track:**
- Emails sent: _____ / 50
- Responses received: _____
- Calls booked: _____ / 20
- Calls completed: _____ / 20

**Conversion rates:**
- Email → Response: _____%
- Response → Call: _____%
- Call → "Yes, would pay": _____%

## Tips

1. **Update immediately after each call** (don't wait)
2. **Use verbatim quotes** (they're gold for messaging)
3. **Color code rows**: Green (Hot), Yellow (Warm), Red (Cold)
4. **Sort by "Would Pay"** to see your best leads first
5. **Filter by industry** to see which vertical is hottest
